Our Story

Baked with patience, served with love.

Maison Dorée was born in a small kitchen on Rue de la Paix, where Élise Fontaine began baking sourdough loaves for her neighbours before the streets had woken up. Today, her granddaughter Claire carries that same spirit — no shortcuts, no preservatives, only time and intention.

We source our heritage grain flours from family farms, our butter from a small dairy in the hills, and our seasonal fruit from the market two blocks away. Every choice is deliberate. Every bite should feel like it was made for you.

Heritage Grains

Stone-milled flour from farms we visit ourselves, every season.

Slow Fermentation

Our sourdoughs rest for 36 hours minimum. Patience is the recipe.

Zero Waste Kitchen

Day-old bread becomes crumbs, croutons, and puddings — nothing is wasted.

Seasonal Menu

Our menu shifts with the seasons. We bake what's best right now.

The Craft

Before the city wakes, we're already baking.

1

Milling & Sourcing

We work with three family farms who stone-mill heritage grain varieties to our specification. Flour arrives weekly, always fresh.

2

Slow Fermentation

Our levain is 14 years old. Doughs cold-ferment overnight — sometimes longer — developing complexity that fast baking can never replicate.

3

Hand Shaping

Every loaf is shaped by hand. We don't use dividers or molds — the imperfection is the point. Each one is a little different, and that's how it should be.

4

Stone Deck Baking

Our imported French stone deck oven reaches 260°C. Bread hits the stone at precisely the right moment — no timer, only touch and intuition.
