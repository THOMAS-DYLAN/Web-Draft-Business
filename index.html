Our story

Flour, time, and a little obsession.

Maison Dorée was born in a tiny rented kitchen in 2009, when Élise Beaumont returned from five years training in Lyon and Paris with one conviction: good bread cannot be rushed. Every loaf begins 48 hours before it reaches your hands — on a living sourdough starter fed daily since our very first week.

We use heritage wheat milled within 50 miles, cultured butters from a single farm, and whatever the season brings. We have never used a bread improver. We never will.

How we work

The craft behind every loaf

01

Mill & Source

Heritage grains milled within 50 miles, ordered weekly. Single-farm cultured butters — no compromises.

02

Long Ferment

All doughs cold-ferment 24–72 hours. No shortcuts. No commercial yeast additives, ever.

03

Hand Shape

Every single loaf shaped by hand. We refuse to let a machine decide the character of your bread.

04

Stone Bake

Deck ovens at 520°F with live steam injection — the only way to achieve the crust we lose sleep over.
