Our Menu

From the first sourdough pulled at 5 AM to the last seasonal tart glazed at noon — everything here is made by hand, with ingredients we'd choose for our own table.

Frequently
Asked

A few things people often want to know before their first visit. If you have something else on your mind, we're always happy to chat in person or by email.

We open at 7 AM Monday through Friday, 6:30 AM on Saturday, and 7 AM on Sunday. Croissants and viennoiseries typically sell out by 10–10:30 AM. Bread lasts a little longer — usually until early afternoon. We strongly recommend arriving before 9 AM if you have your heart set on something specific.

Yes — we accept pre-orders for whole loaves, pastry selections of 6 or more, and all celebration cakes. Use the order form on our contact page, or call us before noon the day prior. Pre-orders are held until 11 AM on collection day.

We bake in a kitchen that handles gluten, dairy, eggs, and nuts, so we cannot guarantee any item is allergen-free. That said, we do offer a dark rye loaf that is dairy-free, and several vegan-friendly options each week. Please speak to us at the counter or contact us in advance.

Leave the cut side face-down on a board at room temperature — this is all you need for 2–3 days. Never refrigerate sourdough; it stales much faster. For longer storage, slice and freeze on day one or two, then toast from frozen. A damp cloth rewarm at 180°C for 8 minutes also works beautifully for a whole loaf.

We require a minimum of 5 days notice for standard celebration cakes, and 10 days for tiered or particularly elaborate designs. We'll arrange a brief consultation to discuss flavours, design and allergens, then a 50% deposit confirms your booking. The remaining balance is collected at collection.

Yes — we supply a small number of local restaurants and cafés with daily bread and a weekly pastry selection. We work best with partners who value slow bread over convenience. If that sounds like you, reach out via our contact page and we'll set up a tasting.
